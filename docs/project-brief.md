# Teal Registry Project Brief

## Positioning

Teal Registry is a public standards and verification layer for Teal organizational claims. It should feel institutional, clear, and trust-forward rather than sales-driven.

Core homepage framing from the client notes:

> Independent verification for how organizations actually work.

Plain-language explanation:

Teal Registry verifies whether organizations operate with clear decision-making, healthy human practices, and adaptive leadership, or are only claiming to.

## Primary Audiences

- Funders and partners checking whether a claim is credible
- Organizations and land/project stewards seeking recognition
- Practitioners and providers navigating credential pathways
- Communities and teams affected by public claims
- Reporters or observers checking misuse

## Primary Jobs

- Verify a Teal claim by entity name, badge ID, or QR link
- Browse the public directory by status, scope, region, type, and sector
- Understand what each status means and what it does not mean
- Read standards, governance, enforcement, appeals, and renewal rules
- Apply for a credential or accreditation pathway
- Report misuse or concerns

## Non-Negotiable Trust Boundaries

- Verification must not be bundled with training, consulting, or implementation.
- Statuses must be precise, scoped, and time-bound.
- Public listings must show "as of" or review dates.
- Badge verification must show current status, scope, expiration/review window, and official listing link.
- Misuse reporting must be visible from registry, profile, and badge verification surfaces.

- Institutional, calm, standards-body credible
- Trust-forward and directory-first
- Strong use of real brand assets, badges, and official marks
- No hype, inflated claims, or personality-led hero
- Mobile-first search and verification flows
